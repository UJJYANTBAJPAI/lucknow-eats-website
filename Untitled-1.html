A Feast for the Eyes

The Legacy of Awadhi Cuisine

Lucknowi cuisine, also known as Awadhi cuisine, is a culinary style with roots in the Mughal cooking techniques. It is known for its elaborate dishes like kebabs, kormas, biryanis, and breads like sheermal and roomali roti. The "dum pukht" or slow-fire cooking method is central to Awadhi cuisine, which allows the flavors of the spices to infuse deeply into the food.

This website is a tribute to the rich culinary heritage of Lucknow, aiming to guide food enthusiasts to the most authentic and beloved eateries in the City of Nawabs.
